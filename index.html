Bubble tea, also known as pearl milk tea, originated in Taiwan in the 1980s. It has since become a popular beverage worldwide, known for its chewy tapioca pearls.

Taiwan is famous for its vibrant night markets, offering a wide variety of street food, snacks, clothing, and games. These markets are a popular destination for both locals and tourists.

Taiwan is home to numerous natural hot springs, known for their therapeutic properties. Some of the most popular hot spring destinations include Beitou in Taipei and Wulai in New Taipei City.

Taiwanese cuisine is diverse and influenced by various cultures, including Chinese, Japanese, and indigenous Taiwanese. Some popular dishes include beef noodle soup, stinky tofu, and oyster omelets.

Taiwan is known for its technology industry, particularly its semiconductor and computer hardware manufacturing. Companies like TSMC, Asus, and Acer are based in Taiwan.

Taiwan is often referred to as a cycling paradise due to its extensive network of cycling paths and routes. The island offers a range of cycling experiences, from scenic coastal rides to challenging mountain climbs.

It has also been reported that Taiwanese students spend more hours at school than any others in the world, with an average of 9.5 hours at school. And this is not even counting the many additional hours they spend at cram schools, tutoring sessions, and other classes.

5-Day Tokyo Itinerary Overview

  • Day 1: Shinjuku, Meiji Shrine, Harajuku, Shibuya
  • Day 2: Ueno, Kitchen Street, Asakusa, Tokyo Skytree
  • Day 3: Japanese Gardens, Imperial Palace, Ginza, Akihabara
  • Day 4: Toyosu or Tsukiji Fish Market, Odaiba, TeamLab Planets, Tokyo Tower
  • Day 5:
    • Option 1: Chic boutiques and cafes in Nakameguro, Daikanyama, and Ebisu
    • Option 2: Upscale art & architecture in Aoyama, Omotesando, Azabudai Hills, and art museums
    • Option 3: Day trip to Hakone & Mt. Fuji
